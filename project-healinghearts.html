Healing Hearts

A healthcare mobile app focused on improving the appointment booking experience for patients managing chronic conditions. The existing flow caused anxiety and drop-offs - people were giving up before they could get help.

32% Drop-off Reduction
12 Usability Test Participants
2 Research Rounds
Overview

The Problem

Patients weren't dropping off because the app was hard to use. They were dropping off because the language felt clinical and cold. The interface triggered anxiety at an already vulnerable moment.

I was brought in to redesign the entire booking flow - from the first tap to the confirmation screen - with a focus on reducing friction and emotional resistance.

Research

What I Found

After interviewing 12 patients across two research rounds, the key insight emerged: the copy was the interface. Visual hierarchy could be perfect, but if the words felt like a hospital form, users would abandon. I redesigned the language alongside the layout.

Process

How I Worked

Research- Define- Design- Test- Ship

End-to-end UX design: discovery research, IA restructure, wireframing, high-fidelity prototyping in Figma, and usability testing with 12 participants across two rounds.

Outcome

What Changed

Appointment booking drop-off reduced significantly. Test participants described the redesigned flow as "reassuring" and "like talking to a human." The design is now live in production.
