the final product

How did designing for AI change the way I think about UX?

When AI is involved, transparency isn't optional — it's the product. Researchers needed to see not just what the model extracted, but how confident it was, and where it might be wrong.

Designing for AI taught me that the human's judgment is still the real product. The interface wasn't replacing the researcher — it was giving them their time back so they could spend it on work that actually needs a human brain.

That shift — from tool to collaborator — is now how I approach every interface that sits between a person and a system.
